महाभारत

An Epic of Ancient India

The Mahabharata is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, the other being the Ramayana. It is a vast and complex narrative of the Kurukshetra War, a dynastic struggle for the throne of Hastinapura between two groups of cousins, the Kauravas and the Pandavas. This interactive guide invites you to explore its core conflict, pivotal characters, and timeless philosophical wisdom.

The Core Conflict

The epic revolves around the intense rivalry between two families of cousins—the Pandavas, representing dharma (righteousness), and the Kauravas, driven by adharma (unrighteousness). Their dispute over the kingdom of Hastinapura escalates from a game of dice to the catastrophic Kurukshetra War.

The Pandavas

The five sons of King Pandu, renowned for their virtue and righteousness. They are the rightful heirs to the throne.

  • Yudhishthira (The Just)
  • Bhima (The Mighty)
  • Arjuna (The Archer)
  • Nakula (The Handsome)
  • Sahadeva (The Wise)

The Kauravas

The one hundred sons of the blind King Dhritarashtra, led by the eldest, the arrogant and envious Duryodhana.

  • Duryodhana (The Antagonist)
  • Dushasana (His cruel brother)
  • And their 98 brothers
  • Supported by figures like Karna
