Ramayana Stories Timeline

Early Life of Rama

Birth of Rama

King Dasharatha conducts the Putrakameshti ritual, resulting in the birth of Rama and his brothers.

Sita’s Swayamvara

Rama earns Sita’s hand by shattering Lord Shiva’s powerful bow.

Kaikeyi’s Boons

Queen Kaikeyi claims her boons, leading to Rama’s 14-year exile.

Bharata’s Devotion

Bharata governs Ayodhya, keeping Rama’s sandals as a symbol, awaiting his return.

Ahalya’s Redemption

Rama frees Ahalya from her curse with a touch of his feet, bringing her back to life.

Exile and Early Encounters

Shurpanakha’s Encounter

Rama and Lakshmana face Ravana's sister, severing her nose when she menaces Sita.

The Golden Deer

Maricha transforms into a golden deer to distract Rama, allowing Ravana to kidnap Sita.

Jatayu’s Sacrifice

The valiant Jatayu battles Ravana to save Sita but suffers mortal wounds.

Sabari’s Berries

Sabari, a devoted elder, shares berries with Rama after tasting them for sweetness.

Rama Meets Hanuman

Hanuman encounters Rama in Kishkindha, forging their timeless connection.

Vali-Sugriva Duel

Rama aids Sugriva in regaining his kingdom by defeating his brother Vali.

Search for Sita

Hanuman’s Leap to Lanka

Hanuman soars over the sea, seeking Sita in distant Lanka.

Hanuman and Lankini

Hanuman overcomes Lanka's guardian spirit to enter the city.

Sita in Ashok Vatika

Hanuman finds Sita in captivity and gives her Rama’s ring as a sign of hope.

Burning of Lanka

Captured Hanuman's tail is ignited, and he uses it to torch parts of Lanka.

Hanuman Delivers the Message

Hanuman delivers Sita’s message to Rama, sparking plans for battle.

War in Lanka

Kumbhakarna’s Battle

Kumbhakarna, Ravana’s massive brother, battles fiercely but falls to Rama.

Meghnad (Indrajit) Defeated

Indrajit, the sorcerous son of Ravana, meets his end at Lakshmana’s hands.

Ahiravan’s Capture

Ahiravan captures Rama and Lakshmana, but Hanuman saves them by killing him in Patala.

Vibheeshana’s Counsel

Vibheeshana, Ravana’s righteous brother, defects and joins Rama’s side.

Hanuman Brings Sanjeevani

To aid Lakshmana, Hanuman carries the Sanjeevani-clad mountain to the battlefield.

Mandodari’s Wisdom

Mandodari, queen of Ravana, urges him to return Sita to prevent disaster, yet he declines.

Ravana’s Final Battle

Rama defeats Ravana using the Brahmastra, bringing peace and upholding dharma.

Return and Aftermath

Return to Ayodhya

Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana return after 14 years; people celebrate with lamps (origin of Diwali).

Agni Pariksha

Sita faces a fiery ordeal to demonstrate her purity to Rama and the world.

Sita in Valmiki’s Ashram

Sita departs Ayodhya and bears Lava and Kusha at Sage Valmiki’s ashram.

Lava and Kusha Encounter

The twins seize Rama’s ceremonial horse, sparking a clash until their true identities are unveiled.

Ashwamedha Yajna

Rama conducts the Ashwamedha ritual, signifying authority, culminating in the Lava-Kusha tale.

Sita Returns to the Earth

Sita, burdened by uncertainty, calls upon Mother Earth, who opens up to embrace her.

Rama’s Departure

Rama ultimately departs his earthly existence, ascending to his celestial realm as Vishnu.

Lesser-Known and Folk Tales

Hanuman and the Sun

As a child, Hanuman sees the sun as a fruit and attempts to eat it.

Hanuman’s Test of Devotion

Hanuman rips his chest apart, revealing Rama and Sita residing in his heart.

Ravana’s Boons and Arrogance

Ravana gains blessings from Shiva and Brahma, yet his pride brings about his ruin.

Hanuman and Bhima Meet

Hanuman imparts wisdom to Bhima, showing him true strength and humility.

Hanuman’s Boon of Immortality

Hanuman receives the boon of immortality, destined to live until time's end.

Hanuman and Rama’s Ring

During the Mahabharata, Hanuman teaches Arjuna humility over Rama’s bridge-building doubts.

Hanuman Meets Lava and Kusha

Hanuman befriends Rama’s sons, Lava and Kusha, at Valmiki’s ashram.

Story of Shabari’s Faith

Her devotion is rewarded by meeting Rama, exemplifying pure bhakti.

Hanuman’s Later Adventures

Hanuman upholds dharma across eras, manifesting in later yugas to safeguard his devotees.

Hanuman’s Encounter with Makardhwaja

Hanuman encounters his son Makardhwaja, born from his sweat, while saving Rama and Lakshmana from Ahiravan.
