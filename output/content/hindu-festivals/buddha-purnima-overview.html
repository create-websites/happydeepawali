The Thrice-Blessed Festival

Buddha Purnima, known globally as Vesak, is uniquely sacred. It commemorates three pivotal events in the life of Gautama Buddha that all occurred on the same day‚Äîthe full moon of the Vaisakha month: his birth, his enlightenment, and his final passing. The Path to Awakening

The life of Siddhartha Gautama is the foundational narrative of Buddhism, a journey from princely luxury to profound wisdom. Each event illustrates a core tenet of the Dharma. Interact with the timeline below to follow his transformative story from birth to final passing.

The Heart of the Dharma

The enlightenment the Buddha attained was a direct understanding of reality and the path to liberation. These teachings, the Dharma, form the philosophical core of Buddhism. Use the tabs below to explore the foundational principles: the Four Noble Truths and the Noble Eightfold Path.

A World of Celebration

While the core meaning of Vesak is universal, its expression is filtered through the rich lens of local culture. From serene candlelight processions in Thailand to Sri Lanka's brilliant festival of lanterns, the day is celebrated in beautifully diverse ways. Select a country to discover its unique traditions.

The Grammar of Devotion

The celebrations of Buddha Purnima are built upon shared devotional acts. These rituals are not empty formalities but are a form of embodied learning, translating abstract concepts into tangible actions that cultivate virtue and wisdom. Explore the symbolism behind these common practices.

When is Vesak?

The date of Vesak is determined by lunisolar calendars, leading to variations worldwide. While most of South & Southeast Asia observe it on the Vaisakha full moon, East Asia often uses the fourth month of the Chinese lunar calendar. Select a year to see the upcoming dates for different traditions.
