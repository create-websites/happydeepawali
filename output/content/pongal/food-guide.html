Quick tips for Sankranti cooking

Use seasonal ingredients
The authentic taste of Sankranti is enhanced by freshly harvested sesame, jaggery, peanuts, and
Balance sweet + savory
Include one sweet (til-gud or coconut ladoo) and one savory (khichdi or ven pongal) for a balanced menu.
Make-ahead strategy
Chikki and ladoos have good shelf life, but for optimal flavor, prepare khichdi or pongal fresh.
Gift-ready packing
Pack line boxes with butter paper and include a sweet Sankranti wish for friends and neighbors.
Menu idea: A full Sankranti meal consists of Til-gud ladoo, coconut ladoo, and either khichdi or ven pongal.

FAQ

Which sweet is most traditional for Makar Sankranti?
Sweets made with til (sesame) and gud (jaggery) are popularly exchanged for their warmth and representation of sweet words and strong relationships.
What are quick recipes for beginners?
Coconut ladoo, til chikki, and basic khichdi are great for beginners and require only a few tools.
Can I make these recipes without refined sugar?
Many recipes feature the use of either jaggery or condensed milk. In the case of coconut ladoo, condensed milk serves as a quick substitution.
