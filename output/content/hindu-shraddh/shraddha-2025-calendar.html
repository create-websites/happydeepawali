Shraddha Calendar & Guide 2025

A handy guide to exploring and discovering Shraddha dates for Pitru Paksha.

Understanding Shraddha and Pitru Paksha

This guide helps you navigate Pitru Paksha, a sacred Hindu period for honoring ancestors. At its heart is the Shraddha ritual, a tribute of gratitude to departed family souls. Included is a 2025 Pitru Paksha calendar and clear steps to identify the right day for these rites, enabling you to uphold traditions with ease and confidence.

How to Find Your Shraddha Date

Identifying the proper date for Shraddha is simple. This guide explains the main rule and highlights key exceptions. The aim is to conduct the ritual on the lunar day (Tithi) linked to the ancestor's death.

The Main Rule: Match the Tithi

The yearly Shraddha ritual for a forebear is conducted on the same. Tithi (lunar day) of their demise, but in the Krishna Paksha (waning moon phase) of Bhadrapada/Ashwin, referred to as Pitru Paksha. For instance, if someone passed on a Panchami Tithi, their Shraddha would occur on the Panchami Tithi noted in the calendar above.

Key Exceptions and Special Days

  • Navami Shraddha: Called Avidhava Navami, this day is dedicated to honoring married women who died before their spouses through Shraddha rituals.
  • Chaturdashi Shraddha: This Tithi is dedicated to individuals who passed away unnaturally, including accidents, suicides, or homicides.
  • Sarva Pitru Amavasya: This day holds great importance. If you're unaware of an ancestor's Tithi or missed the exact day, you can honor all ancestors with Shraddha on the last day of Pitru Paksha.

Significance of the Ritual

Shraddha transcends mere ritual; it is a heartfelt expression of gratitude and remembrance. As per Hindu tradition, performing these rites during Pitru Paksha aids ancestral souls in attaining peace and progressing in their spiritual journey. In return, ancestors are believed to bless their family with health, wealth, and prosperity. This sacred observance deepens familial ties beyond the material world, nurturing a timeless bond with one's heritage and lineage.
