How to Find Your Shraddha Date: A Simple Guide

Determining the right day is easy using the lunar day, or Tithi, of an ancestor's death. This chart outlines the basic steps to follow.

Identify the Ancestor's Tithi

Find the lunar day (e.g., Panchami, Dashami) of their passing.

Check for Special Cases

Was the death unnatural? (Refer to Chaturdashi). Did she pass before her husband? (Refer to Navami).

Match the Tithi

When no exceptions exist, locate the matching Tithi in the Pitru Paksha calendar and carry out Shraddha accordingly.

Unknown Tithi?

If the Tithi is unclear or the date is missed, honor all ancestors on Sarva Pitru Amavasya.

The Spiritual Significance

Pitru Paksha serves as a holy link between generations, where Shraddha rituals express deep gratitude, offering peace to ancestral souls and inviting their blessings for health, wealth, and harmony. It’s a moment to celebrate our heritage and reflect on the foundation of our identity.
