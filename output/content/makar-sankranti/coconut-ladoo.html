Coconut ladoo recipe

Ingredients and directions from your original page.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry coconut powder
  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 3/4 cup sugar powder
  • 5-6 teaspoons boiled milk
  • Raisins or badam for garnish

Directions

  1. Combine coconut powder, milk powder, and sugar powder in a mixing bowl.
  2. Slowly add milk and bind the mixture together.
  3. Shape the mixture into laddoos.
  4. Top each laddoo with a raisin or badam before serving to complete your coconut ladoos.
Festival twist: Include cardamom powder and a couple of saffron strands to achieve the traditional Pongal fragrance.

Coconut ladoo for Sankranti & Pongal

Why coconut ladoo for Sankranti?

While sesame-jaggery sweets are traditional Sankranti treats, coconut ladoo is just as popular for its comforting, nutritious, and convenient qualities at winter get-togethers.

Why coconut ladoo for Pongal?

Pongal is a celebration of the harvest season, during which sweet dishes are offered as prasadam. Coconut plays a prominent role in South Indian cuisine, making coconut ladoo a popular festive treat enjoyed alongside Sakkarai Pongal.

Best time to make

Tips & storage

Binding tip
Gradually pour in the milk to avoid making the mixture too sticky. If it becomes too wet, simply mix in a little more coconut powder.
Texture tip
Lightly roast the coconut to enhance its nutty aroma, being careful not to over-brown it.
Storage
Keep in an airtight container. Store at room temperature for 1-2 days in cool weather or in the fridge for up to 5-7 days. Remember to bring to room temperature before serving.
