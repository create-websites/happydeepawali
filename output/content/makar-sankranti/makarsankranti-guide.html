Sun Transition

“Makar Sankranti” comes from Makara (Capricorn) + Sankranti (transition). The day is also called Uttarayan as the sun starts its trek northward, signaling the approach of longer days.

Multiple Names

  • Pongal
  • Uttaryan
  • Magh Bihu
  • Khichdi Purv

A celebration of feasting, gathering with family, and giving thanks that is observed in India and beyond.

Farmers and Harvesting

Makar Sankranti often coincides with the completion of winter harvest. Families offer gratitude for the bounty of the fields and seek good fortune for future crops.

Worship Cows

In many regions, Go Puja Paying tribute to cows and bulls for their contributions to agriculture, and sending prayers for their well-being and the success of future harvests.
