Directions
  1. Wash the rice and soak it in water for 1‚Äì2 hours.
  2. Bring sugarcane juice to a boil in a pan.
  3. Once it reaches a boil, stir in the soaked rice and cardamom. Let it simmer on low heat. slow flame.

Tips for best taste

Small things that make sugarcane kheer smoother and more fragrant.

Keep it low & slow

Gently simmer on low heat to maintain the natural sugarcane taste and avoid excessive caramelization.

Stir often

Rice can settle‚Äîstirring keeps the texture smooth and avoids sticking.

Add aroma at the right time

Include cardamom while simmering; add dried fruits towards the end to maintain their crunch.

Serving ideas

Serve warm for cozy winter evenings, or chill and garnish with pistachios for a refreshing dessert. Pair it with til-gud laddoos or gajak for a complete Sankranti sweet plate.
