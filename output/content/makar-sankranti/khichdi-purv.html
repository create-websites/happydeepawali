Description of Khichdi-purvKhichdi-purv is a regional festival celebrated in certain parts of India, which coincides with the festival of Makar Sankranti. It is named after 'khichdi,' a dish made from rice and lentils, which is commonly prepared and consumed during this festival.
Relation to SankrantiKhichdi-purv is a regional term used for the celebration of Makar Sankranti, which signifies the sun's move into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara in Sanskrit) and is linked to the harvest time.
Significance of Khichdi-purvThe festival marks the start of the harvest season and the conclusion of the winter solstice. Consuming khichdi on this occasion is thought to bring well-being, wealth, and harmony. It is also a time for communal gatherings and showing reverence to the deities, especially the Sun god.
How it is celebratedFestivities involve making and enjoying khichdi, bathing in rivers, flying kites, and giving to the less fortunate. Additionally, individuals visit temples, conduct prayers, and set goals for the upcoming year.
Difference from Makar SankrantiAlthough Khichdi-purv is focused on the making and eating of khichdi, Makar Sankranti encompasses a wide range of regional customs, including activities like flying kites, lighting bonfires, and enjoying feasts with various dishes other than khichdi.

Why is khichdi so important on Sankranti?

Wholesome winter food

Rice and lentils offer warmth, energy, and easy digestion, making them perfect for winter meals.

Harvest symbolism

Showing appreciation for nature's abundance by using freshly harvested grains and pulses.

Community sharing

A one-pot meal is simple to prepare in bulk, ideal for serving langar-style community feasts.

Tip: Several families also prepare sesame sweets with khichdi, a traditional Sankranti combination.

How people celebrate Khichdi Parv

1
Early morning
Numerous worshipers immerse themselves in rivers like the Ganga, Yamuna, and Sarayu before offering prayers to Surya, the Sun god.
2
Puja & offerings
Families often offer khichdi, sesame-jaggery sweets, and seasonal produce as charity (daan) is a common practice.
3
Community meals
Khichdi is prepared in both households and communal kitchens, where it is enjoyed by neighbors and guests alike.
4
Fairs & gatherings
A significant Khichdi Mela is held in Gorakhpur, particularly during Makar Sankranti.

FAQ

Is Khichdi Parv the same as Makar Sankranti?
Makar Sankranti is known as Khichdi Parv in Uttar Pradesh and surrounding areas due to the significance of khichdi in the festivities.
Where is it celebrated most?
The festival is closely tied to Uttar Pradesh, parts of Bihar, and nearby regions, with various communities celebrating it in their unique ways.
What is Khichdi Mela?
A major public event and pilgrimage celebrated during Makar Sankranti in certain locations, such as Gorakhpur, where worshippers present khichdi at Gorakhnath Temple.
