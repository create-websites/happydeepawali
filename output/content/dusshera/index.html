Building the Giant

The heart of the celebration is the effigy itself. This section provides a step-by-step guide to constructing the effigy of Ravana. Use the tabs below to navigate through each phase of the build, from creating the sturdy bamboo frame to the final decorative touches. Each step includes key materials and expert tips for a spectacular result.

1. The Bamboo Skeleton

The frame is the most critical part, providing structure and stability. Use strong, thick bamboo for the main torso and legs, and thinner pieces for arms and details. Lash all joints securely with rope or strong wire.

  • Materials: Thick bamboo poles, thin bamboo sticks, strong coir rope, wire.
  • Tip: Create a wide, stable base to prevent the effigy from tipping over, especially in windy conditions.
  • Action: Construct the torso first, then attach the legs, arms, and neck structure. Ensure everything is symmetrical.

2. Creating the Skin

Once the frame is ready, it's time to give it shape and a surface to paint on. This is typically done with paper, cardboard, or cloth stretched over the frame.

  • Materials: Old newspapers, colored paper, cardboard, strong adhesive (flour paste), hay/straw.
  • Tip: Use hay or straw to bulk out the chest and limbs before applying the paper layers. This gives the effigy a more three-dimensional shape.
  • Action: Apply multiple layers of paper using adhesive for strength. Allow each layer to dry completely.

3. Crafting the Ten Heads

Ravana's ten heads are his defining feature. Each head can be crafted separately and then attached to the main structure. Pay attention to the facial expressions to make them look menacing.

  • Materials: Clay, papier-mâché, cardboard, paints.
  • Tip: Create a main, larger head for the center and nine smaller, identical heads to arrange around it. A lightweight material like papier-mâché is ideal.
  • Action: Mold the faces, let them dry, then paint them with bright, bold colors. Don't forget the iconic mustache!

4. Final Paint and Fireworks

The final step is to bring the effigy to life with paint and prepare it for the main event by carefully placing fireworks inside.

  • Materials: Brightly colored paints, decorative cloths, fireworks, crackers.
  • Tip: Paint the body with traditional attire and ornaments. Use gold and black to highlight features.
  • Action: Carefully place fireworks inside the body and head cavities, ensuring they are secure. This should ONLY be done by experienced adults.

Safety First, Always!

Ravan Dahan involves fire and fireworks, making safety the absolute top priority. This section outlines essential safety protocols to ensure a hazard-free celebration for everyone involved. Following these guidelines is not optional—it is crucial for protecting the construction team, the audience, and the surrounding property.

👷‍♀️ Construction Site

  • Ensure a stable, level base.
  • Wear gloves when handling bamboo/wire.
  • Keep the area clear of debris.

🎆 Firework Handling

  • Only adults should handle fireworks.
  • Store crackers in a cool, dry place.
  • Do not use damaged or dud fireworks.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Crowd Control

  • Establish a safe viewing distance.
  • Use barriers or ropes.
  • Have clear entry and exit points.

🧯 Emergency Prep

  • Keep fire extinguishers handy.
  • Have buckets of sand and water ready.
  • Know emergency contact numbers.
