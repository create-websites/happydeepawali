Green Dussehra: Celebrating with Mother Nature in Mind

As we honor the victory of good over evil, let's extend that triumph to safeguarding our planet. An eco-friendly Dussehra enriches the festive spirit with mindful choices that nurture Earth. From green effigies to waste-free celebrations, let's make this Dussehra a win for the environment as well.

Crafting Sustainable Effigies

The traditional burning of Ravana's effigy contributes to air pollution. Here's how to make it eco-friendly:

  • Natural Materials: Opt for bamboo, hay, jute ropes, and reused paper over plastic, foam, or artificial textiles.
  • Non-Toxic Colors: Adorn the effigies using organic, toxin-free hues such as turmeric, rice flour, and flower-derived pigments.
  • Go Cracker-Free: Refrain from filling effigies with dangerous fireworks that emit toxic smoke and create excessive noise.
  • Symbolic Size: Opt for smaller effigies to reduce material consumption and emissions.

Reducing Festive Waste

Festivals can produce substantial waste. Embrace eco-friendly habits to reduce our impact.

  • Ditch Plastic Decor: Decorate with fresh blooms, mango leaves, and earthen diyas rather than plastic banners and electric lights.
  • Eco-Friendly Feasts: Use banana leaves or steel plates for serving food. Skip single-use plastic cups and cutlery.
  • Compost Food Waste: Create a compost system for food scraps and organic waste from feasts and pujas.
  • Community Clean-up: Plan a post-event cleanup to keep public areas tidy after the festivities.

Community & Collective Action

Sustainable celebrations unite communities, fostering connections and amplifying positive change.

  • One Community Effigy: Combine efforts to create a single community effigy from sustainable materials, rather than several smaller ones in the neighborhood.
  • Green Workshops: Host sessions on crafting sustainable decor, clay sculptures, and eco-friendly dyes.
  • Gift Saplings: Swap saplings as celebratory gifts to encourage tree planting and a sustainable future.
  • Spread Awareness: Leverage the festival to spark conversations on eco-friendly topics and encourage sustainable habits.
