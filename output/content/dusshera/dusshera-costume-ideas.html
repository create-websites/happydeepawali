Dussehra & Ramlila Costume Ideas

Bring the timeless tale of Ramayana alive with these imaginative costume ideas for your Ramlila play or festive Dussehra event.

Lord Rama

  • Attire: A saffron or yellow dhoti paired with a coordinating sash or scarf draped over the shoulders.
  • Jewelry: Plain gold-toned bracelets, bangles, and a pendant. A modest crown or headdress (mukut).
  • Props: A dhanush (bow) and tarkash (quiver) are indispensable.
  • Makeup: A calm demeanor, a crescent-shaped tilak, and delicate eye accents.

Sita

  • Attire: A graceful and refined saree, often in hues of peach, coral, or gold.
  • Jewelry: Floral ornaments (Pushpa Abushan) for Ayodhya scenes, and simple, minimal pieces for the exile era.
  • Hairstyle: Hair styled in a sleek bun or flowing braid, decorated with blooms.
  • Makeup: Elegant and effortless makeup, accented by a red bindi and defined eyes.

Lakshmana

  • Attire: Like Rama, a dhoti paired with an angavastram, maybe in soft cream or pale orange hues.
  • Jewelry: Minimalistic jewelry to show his dedication and service.
  • Props: Wields a bow and arrow, representing his duty as a guardian.
  • Makeup: A tilak like Rama's, paired with a focused and resolute gaze.

Hanuman

  • Attire: A red or orange dhoti or loincloth (langot). A holy thread (janeu) draped across the chest.
  • Accessories: A monkey face is essential. A fabric or rope tail completes the look. A basic crown ties it together.
  • Props: A mace (gada) is Hanuman's signature weapon.
  • Makeup: If not using a mask, face paint can create a monkey-like appearance.

Ravana

  • Attire: A majestic, deep-toned dhoti (black, navy, or violet) paired with an ornate angavastram.
  • Jewelry: Elaborate and striking golden adornmentsâ€”bold pendants, bangles, and an opulent tiara.
  • Key Feature: Nine cardboard heads fanned out behind the lead performer.
  • Props: A blade or a trident weapon. A striking, curled mustache (natural or artificial).

Vanar Sena

  • Attire: Simple brown, red, or orange shorts or dhotis.
  • Accessories: Monkey masks or face paint. Fabric tails clipped on.
  • Props: Tiny toy clubs or rods. Others might wield big cardboard "stones" labeled "Ram."
  • Group Idea: Simple and enjoyable for kids to wear as a group.

General DIY & Makeup Tips

  • Fabrics: Opt for affordable fabrics like cotton for dhotis and sarees. Glossy or brocade materials suit Ravana's attire.
  • Jewelry: Golden-painted cardboard, sparkly beads, and ribbon make perfect DIY crowns or decorations.
  • Weapons & Props: Cardboard can be crafted into bows, arrows, maces, or swords, then painted and adorned with glitter or foil.
  • Makeup: Opt for safe face paint. Combine a base shade, kohl for eyes, and a bindi or tilak to create a striking character look.
