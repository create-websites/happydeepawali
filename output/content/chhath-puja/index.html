The Cycle of Devotion

Chhath is more than just a festival - it is a four-day period of penance marked by a strict progression of purification, fasting, and offerings, starting with cleaning the home and culminating in standing in river waters.

Day 1

Nahay Khay

Worshippers cleanse themselves in sacred water and eat a simple meal of bottle gourd (kaddu) and rice to start the purification process.

Day 2

Kharna

A day-long waterless fast, broken in the evening with Rasiya (kheer) and Roti, marking the beginning of a 36-hour fast.

Day 3

Sandhya Arghya

The main ritual. Offering 'Arghya' (water/milk) to the setting sun while standing in a river or pond.

Day 4

Usha Arghya

Offering 'Arghya' to the rising sunThis signifies the end of the fast and the sharing of Prasad.
