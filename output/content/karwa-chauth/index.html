"Karwa Chauth: A Symbol of Love and Devotion"


Karwa Chauth Festival

Karwa Chauth is a Hindu festival celebrated by married women in India. It falls on the fourth day after the full moon in the Hindu month of Kartik. On this day, married women fast from sunrise to moonrise for the long life and well-being of their husbands.

Significance

Karwa Chauth is a symbol of love and devotion between a husband and wife. It is believed that by fasting and performing puja on this day, a wife can protect her husband from any harm and ensure his long life. It is also a way for married women to show their love and respect for their husbands.

Puja Method

The puja for Karwa Chauth involves worshipping Lord Shiva, Goddess Parvati, and Lord Ganesha. Married women dress up in traditional attire and apply henna on their hands. They then gather with other women in their community and perform the puja together. The puja involves lighting a diya, offering flowers, fruits, and sweets to the deities, and reciting prayers and mantras.

Significance for Newly Married People

Karwa Chauth is especially significant for newly married couples as it is their first Karwa Chauth together. It is a way for them to strengthen their bond and show their love and commitment to each other. The wife's fast is seen as a test of her love and devotion for her husband, and the husband's support and care during the fast is seen as a symbol of his love and respect for his wife.
